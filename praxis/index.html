Why PRAXIS Exists

Practitioner-built,
not prompt-engineered

I spent 12 years writing evaluation designs by hand. Adapting methods for contexts where the textbook doesn't apply: fragile states, conflict zones, settings where an RCT is neither feasible nor ethical. That accumulated knowledge is what PRAXIS encodes.

PRAXIS is not a chatbot wrapper. It's a structured methodology grounded in field-tested approaches, validated psychometric instruments, and the kind of contextual judgment that only comes from doing the work. The AI is the delivery mechanism. The expertise is the product.

Every component is free and open-source. Every tool runs in your browser with zero data transmitted to any server. The evaluation community deserves infrastructure it can trust, inspect, and adapt.

PRAXIS Health

Sector-specific depth, starting with health

4,300+ lines of health-specific evaluation methodology. Seven sub-skills covering the full spectrum of global health evaluation, from health systems strengthening to emergency nutrition response. PVE and Climate modules are next.

WHO building blocks, HSS evaluation, RSSH, systems thinking approaches.

Epidemiology

Study design, surveillance, outbreak investigation, causal inference methods.

CEA, CUA, budget impact analysis, value for money frameworks.

Disease Programmes

RMNCAH & Nutrition
