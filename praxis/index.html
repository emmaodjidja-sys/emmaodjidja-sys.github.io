Why PRAXIS Exists

The practitioner-sovereign alternative

Every other AI for Good initiative runs through institutions. ITU through the UN system. DataKind through corporate volunteers. J-PAL through MIT. The power sits with the institution.

PRAXIS inverts this. Client-side processing. MIT license. No accounts. Offline-capable. No data transmission. The practitioner owns the methodology, the tools, and the output.

That's not a feature set. That's a political position about who owns rigorous methodology in the development sector.

Built on 12 years of writing evaluation designs by hand, adapting methods for contexts where the textbook doesn't apply: fragile states, conflict zones, settings where an RCT is neither feasible nor ethical. That accumulated knowledge is what PRAXIS encodes.

Practitioner AI

AI that augments judgment,
not replaces it.

DataKind's paradigm: AI does data science for you. The corporate paradigm: AI automates your tasks. PRAXIS proposes something different: AI augments professional judgment within structured methodology workflows. The human makes every decision. The AI handles the mechanical drafting between decisions.

Step 1

The practitioner decides

Evaluation questions, design choices, contextual judgments. Every substantive decision belongs to the evaluator.

Step 2

The AI drafts

Instruments, matrices, sampling frameworks, analysis plans. Mechanical production within methodological guardrails.

Step 3

The practitioner overrides

Where contextual knowledge outperforms algorithmic recommendation. The final word is always human.

Better evaluations.
Better evidence. Better outcomes.

PRAXIS is free, open-source, and practitioner-owned. Every tool, every module, every methodology file.
